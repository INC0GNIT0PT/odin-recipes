Belouras

Description

Beloura, bolacho, farinhato, pilouco ou bica are the different names given to this delicacy from traditional Portuguese cuisine, more specifically from the Minho region in northern Portugal.

Belouras are made from wheat, maize and rye flour, mixed with pig's blood and seasoned with strong spices. They should always be cut into thin slices, fried and served immediately, piping hot.

Traditionally, belouras are part of the recipe for Rojões à moda do Minho, but they are an excellent accompaniment to other dishes or appetisers.

Ingredients

  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 orange leaf
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 sprig of parsley
  • 10g baker's yeast
  • 125g rye flour
  • 125g wheat flour
  • 250g cornflour
  • 70g liquid pig's blood
  • meat cooking water
  • pingue (fat from cooking the rojões)
  • salt, pepper and cumin as needed

Steps

  1. Mix the flours and sieve.
  2. Knead them with the baker's yeast, chilli, cumin, blood and enough meat cooking water to make a stiff dough.
  3. Leave to rise in a warm place until it cracks.
  4. Shape the dough into a cylindrical form with wet hands.
  5. Cook the belouras in boiling water, seasoned with salt, parsley, garlic, orange leaves and bay leaves. As soon as they come to the surface, they are cooked.
  6. Remove the belouras and place them on a baking tray, muffled between two cloths.
  7. When it's time to serve, cut them into thin slices and fry in the dripping.
