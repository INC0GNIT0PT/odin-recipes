Rojões à moda do Minho

Description

Rojões à moda do Minho, a traditional Minho cuisine recipe much appreciated by lovers of Portuguese gastronomy.

Dare to try it.

Note:

Rojões a moda do Minho are served on a platter with roast potatoes and garnished with slices of lemon and sprigs of parsley.

Ingredients

  • 800g pork leg without skin but with fat
  • 3.5dl green white wine
  • 3 tablespoons lard
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 dessert spoon sweet paprika or paprika
  • 20 roasted chestnuts
  • 350g belouras
  • 100g pork liver
  • 100g boiled pig's blood
  • white pepper as needed
  • salt to taste as needed

Floured tripe

  • 350g fresh pork casing
  • cornflour
  • cumin
  • 2 lemon

Steps

  1. Cut the pork into cubes about 10 cm square and marinate for 2 hours with the wine, crushed garlic cloves, salt, pepper and bay leaf.
  2. Bring to the boil (preferably in an iron pan) and cook over a high heat until the wine has evaporated.
  3. Over a low heat, add the lard and cook the rojões until they're golden brown.
  4. Then add the paprika dissolved in a little green wine to the sauce.

Floured tripe

  1. Fresh tripe is washed very well and then soaked overnight in water with slices of lemon.
  2. The water should be changed several times during this process.
  3. They are then dried, turned inside out and rubbed with cornflour seasoned with pepper and cumin.
  4. Tie one end and turn inside out. Dip them in boiling water to cook them quickly. Once cooked, they are ready to fry.

Preparation

  1. Pour some of the fat from cooking the rojões into a pan and fry, little by little, the floured tripe cut into 3 cm to 5 cm pieces, the belouras cut into 0.5 cm thick slices and the liver and blood cut into slices.
  2. As these ingredients finish frying, they are added to the rojões to keep everything warm.
  3. The roasted chestnuts are also added, after being peeled.
