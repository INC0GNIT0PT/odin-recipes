Lasagna

Description

Lasagne is a type of pasta, possibly one of the oldest types, made in very wide, flat sheets. The same-named Italian dish is made of stacked layers of lasagna alternating with fillings such as ragù (ground meats and tomato sauce), béchamel sauce, vegetables, cheeses (which may include ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan), and seasonings and spices.

Ingredients

  • 1 packet lasagne
  • 3 yellow onions
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 stalk celery
  • 1 carrot
  • 600g ground beef
  • 800g tomato purée
  • 15cl water
  • 20 cl red wine
  • 2 bay leaves
  • thyme
  • basil
  • 3 pinches grated nutmeg
  • 70g grated cheese
  • 125g Parmesan cheese
  • salt
  • pepper
  • 100g flour
  • 125g butter
  • 1l milk

Steps

  1. Sauté chopped garlic cloves and minced onions in a little olive oil.
  2. Add the chopped carrot and celery stalk, then the meat and sauté.
  3. After a few minutes, add the red wine. Cook until evaporated.
  4. Add tomato purée, water and herbs. Season with salt and pepper, then simmer over low heat for 45 minutes.
  5. Prepare the béchamel sauce: melt 100 g butter.
  6. Remove from heat and add flour all at once.
  7. Return to the heat and stir with a whisk until smooth.
  8. Gradually add the milk.
  9. Stir constantly, until the mixture thickens.
  10. Season with nutmeg, salt and pepper. Cook, stirring, over very low heat for about 5 minutes. Set aside.
  11. Preheat oven to 200°C (gas mark 6-7). Oil the lasagne dish. Top with a thin layer of béchamel sauce, then lasagne sheets, bolognese, béchamel sauce and Parmesan. Repeat 3 times in succession.
  12. On the last layer of lasagne, place only the béchamel sauce and cover with grated cheese. Sprinkle with a few knobs of butter.
  13. Bake in the oven for approximately 25 minutes.
  14. Serve
